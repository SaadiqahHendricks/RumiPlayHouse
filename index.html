Who Are We?

Rumi Playhouse offers an intimate early childhood program serving children ages 2 to 5 years in Athlone, Cape Town and its surrounding areas. We keep our spaces warm, earthy and “homely,” providing a safe and loving environment to each child.

Inspired by the Reggio Emilia approach, we aim not only to care, guide and establish trust but to inspire, to nurture curiosity and to challenge young children.

We value open ended play, connection and the ever evolving interest and development of children.

Although we focus on play based learning, we incorporate the Montessori philosophy but place strong focus on the Reggio Emilia method by providing many unconventional materials – indoors and outdoors as well as in the Atelier (Art/Sensory room).

Our Vision

Our key area of focus is on developing the child as a whole, in a holistic manner by enhancing their self-esteem, providing a second home, security and establishing confidence required for a successful life.

We use minimal transitions and appreciate the gift of time, allowing children to become invested in meaningful interactions with materials, processes, peers and teachers. Above all, we value joy, wonder, and connection in early childhood, and we strive to make these present in all we do.

Our Program

At Rumi Playhouse, we begin our day with work time in the main classroom and in the atelier. Children are invited to move freely about the space while collaborating with peers or engaging in parallel work. Our indoor classroom boasts a community table for bread baking and other group projects, a loose parts center, a block area, a home life corner, a reading nook, and other areas to explore, connect, and create.

We have a separate on-site atelier where children work with light/shadows, sensory materials, and a variety of child-accessible artistic media. As we near the end of work time, children take supervised trips to the bathroom in pairs so as not to disrupt the focused energy of the group. When this is complete, the children gather on the main rug for circle time. We come together to share conversation, to sing, read and learn together but to sweetly be in one another's company.

During circle time, we give children exposure to live music and verse, as we believe they are powerful tools for social/emotional learning, language acquisition, and early math and literacy skills. On Fridays, we offer Islamic studies. When we've finished meeting as a group, the children enjoy a snack and then head outdoors to play, to garden and experience unconventional methods of play. We use the outdoor space in all types of (safe) weather.

Just as in the preschools of Reggio Emilia, Italy, our curriculum is emergent and is based entirely on the interests of the group. Teachers observe children and document the learning process in order to bring new challenges to the students and inform the emergent curriculum. An occupational therapy assessment takes place once the child is enrolled.

We have a clear and predictable structure to our day, with plenty of time for children to exercise interest and agency in our environment. Class sizes are kept small (up to 20 children and 2–3 teachers daily) to ensure responsive relationships and intimate learning experiences. We provide children with space, time, community, and an intentionally-curated environment in which to learn and grow.
